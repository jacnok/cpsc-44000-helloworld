My Favorite Hobby / Activity

There are a few that I am prone to enjoy, but I think right now the easiest one would be to talk about my fascination with digital media. Like computer science, it is not a monolith, but a plethora of different areas covering different concepts such as: photography, videography, motion graphics, and graphic design. Although they all seem similar in approach, they remain different ideas - videography can go hand in hand with motion graphics, but one may live without the other. Good typography is essential to both motion graphics and graphic design, but not required by either. If there was one way to explain the differences, it would be through the avenue of story telling - both photography and graphic design must tell their stories in a single frame, which requires that frame to be perfected - whereas videography and motion graphics have the flexibility to spread their stories out through multiple frames.

Fun Fact

In my prime, I took two years of my life and poured it into a student organization that my college administration was highly unsupportive of, due to the actions of previous leadership. However, they hadn't quite met me yet - between my team and myself, we went from a two-person club to a forty-person club, managing to address every major criticism hurled our way, thus dispelling any reservations that we were not "up to the task". By the time I left, the club that nobody seemed to originally want was crowned the "Club of the Year" by the same administration who had once preferred it fade into obscurity. We held an event with a CEO, we collaborated with other groups across campus, and we even invited outside college organizations to our events... which is how I met the Lewis E-Sports club for the first time.

With this level of success, my nickname on campus ended up being "Mr. President" for the next two years.
